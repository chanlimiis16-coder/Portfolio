Reflection

I really like how these lessons showed me two important sides of teaching. First, I learned about non-digital instructional materials like writing boards, flip charts, and posters. I realized that even simple materials are powerful tools for communicating information and making learning stick. The lessons taught me key rules for designing these, like Unity (using only one idea for each visual) and Legibility (making sure the text is big and readable). It's good to know that the conventional stuff is still very important for an effective classroom.

I also learned about how to use ICT tools to make teaching transformative. I'm excited about using QR codes because they're a quick way to share resources, give instructions, or set up fun activities like a scavenger hunt. I also think Infographics are great. They are visual representations of information or data that can simplify a complex concept or present survey data, which I know will help my future students understand difficult topics easily. Tools like Canva can help me create them, which is a very useful skill to have.

Finally, the lesson on the e-portfolio was really helpful. I now know that an e-portfolio is a digital collection of a student’s work that showcases learning progress, skills, and accomplishments. I like this tool because it encourages students to develop self-assessment skills, provides a platform to showcase talents, and fosters reflection on learning experiences. Understanding these digital tools from QR codes to e-portfolios is a critical step for me to integrate technology into teaching and learning and become a great teacher.
